About

Ayeye thipe-akerte ‘Arrernte bird stories’ has been created by Therese Ryder and Margaret Carew through the Arrernte Language Office at Batchelor Institute, Alice Springs.

Therese was a respected Arrernte artist, well-known for her watercolour landscapes. She was also a language teacher with a long career in Arrernte education and resource production.

Credits and rights

The cultural knowledge and practices described and presented on this site are protected by Indigenous Cultural and Intellectual Property (ICIP) rights. ICIP covers Indigenous Peoples rights to maintain, control, protect and develop their cultural heritage, traditional knowledge and traditional cultural expression.

Respecting ICIP rights means that users of this site will respect the knowledge and cultural material presented here. Respecting the rights includes contacting the rights holders (via the publisher) regarding any intended reuse of material, appropriate attribution when reusing material with consent, and preventing offensive, misleading or insulting use. For more information about ICIP, see the Arts Law information sheet.
